Over 100 Years of Power, Performance, and Innovation

Explore the iconic cars that have defined the legacy of Dodge.

Dodge Brothers Touring Car (1915)

The Dodge Brothers Motor Company was founded in 1914, and by 1915, the Dodge Brothers Touring Car became a symbol of reliability and durability. Its rugged construction helped set the foundation for the American automotive industry.

Dodge Charger (1966)

The Dodge Charger, introduced in 1966, quickly became synonymous with American muscle cars. Known for its fastback design and roaring V8 engines, the Charger became an icon on both the road and racetrack.

Dodge Challenger (1970)

Launched in 1970, the Dodge Challenger represented the height of muscle car culture. With its aggressive styling and range of powerful engines, the Challenger became a cultural symbol of performance.

Dodge Viper (1992)

In 1992, Dodge unleashed the Viper, a true American supercar. With its 8.0L V10 engine, the Viper delivered exceptional performance, offering an exhilarating driving experience unlike any other at the time.
